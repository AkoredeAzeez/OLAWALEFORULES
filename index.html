About Olawale

           
           
               

I am Olawale Ayoola, a penultimate student of Electrical and Electronics Engineering at the University of Lagos. Over the past year, I’ve had the honor of serving as the Welfare Secretary of the Faculty of Engineering, where I led several impactful initiatives. I have played significant roles in several faculty teams including PAADC, ULES ARB, and ECX. Beyond student leadership, I am also a product designer at Getcova technologies, passionate about using technology to solve real problems. I bring together experience, empathy, and execution. I have a strong belief that leadership should be people-centered, transparent, and action-driven. Now, I’m stepping forward to serve as your Faculty President, committed to building a faculty where every student feels supported, heard, and inspired to succeed.

Vision & Plans

               
               
                   

To lead the Faculty of Engineering with integrity, purpose, and innovation, ensuring every student is seen, heard, and supported.

               
               
                   

My Plans for the Faculty

                   
                           
  • Continuity: Ensure the success of established events like Sport week, Faculty week, and the newsletter.
    •                        
  • Finalizing the water project: Work to complete the water project for the faculty (if not completed by the end of this session).
    •                        
  • Town Hall Meetings: Create open forums for students to voice their concerns and ideas directly.
    •                        
  • Doors of Opportunity: Create a platform to share local and international scholarship, grant, and internship opportunities.
    •                        
  • Strengthen ULES involvement in sub-bodies: Foster collaboration and communication between the main student body and its sub-groups.
    •                        
  • Work on release of results: Advocate for the timely release of results before they are uploaded to the portal.
    •                        
  • ULS Website: Develop a dedicated website for the student body to centralize information and resources.
